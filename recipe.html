Quick Hummus Recipe

This recipe makes quick, tasty hummus with no fuss. It’s adapted from several recipes I’ve come across over the years.

Hummus is a delicious thick paste popular in Greek and Middle Eastern cuisine. It pairs beautifully with salad, grilled meats, and warm pita bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas (garbanzo beans)
  • 175g tahini
  • 6 sundried tomatoes
  • Half a red pepper
  • A pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • A dash of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Remove the skin from the garlic and chop coarsely.
  2. Remove all the seeds and stalk from the red pepper, then chop coarsely.
  3. Add all ingredients into a food processor.
  4. Blend until a paste forms:
    • For a coarse “chunky” hummus, blend briefly.
    • For a smooth hummus, blend longer.

Variations

For a different flavor, try blending in a small amount of:

  • Lemon and coriander
  • Chili pepper
  • Lime and chipotle
  • Harissa and mint
  • Spinach and feta cheese

Experiment and see what works for you!

Storage

  • Refrigerate in a sealed container and consume within one week.
  • If it starts to become fizzy, discard it immediately.
  • Hummus can be frozen for up to two months — thaw before using.
